KEY FEATURES
================================================================================

Pause Detection:
  - Window: 200-400ms of silence (natural pause, not turn-end)
  - Turn-end threshold: >700ms suppresses backchannel
  - RMS energy-based (uses existing _audio_rms method)

Constraint System:
  - AI grace period: 3 seconds after AI finishes speaking
  - Prospect grace period: 2 seconds after prospect starts speaking
  - Cooldown: 8-15 seconds (randomized) between backchannels
  - All constraints checked before approval

Selection Algorithm:
  - Probabilistic: "Mm-hmm" (60%) > "Right" (20%) > "Yeah" (15%) > "Okay" (5%)
  - Uses Python's random.choices with weights
  - Varies response naturally

Audio Quality:
  - Pre-synthesized during dial time (parallel with other caches)
  - Same TTS voice as main conversation
  - No fades/compression (minimal processing)
  - 16kHz 16-bit PCM mono (standard format)
  - Ultra-short clips (~100-300ms each)

VAD Integration:
  - Does NOT reset silence manager (correct!)
  - Does NOT reset turn timers
  - Treated as "micro-response" not full turn
  - Yields silence frame to Deepgram (doesn't interrupt STT)
  - Uses _ai_speaking flag for echo suppression timing only

Logging:
  - backchannel_enabled / disabled
  - backchannel_audio_loaded (per type)
  - backchannel_injectable_pause_detected
  - backchannel_selected
  - backchannel_suppressed_* (various reasons)
  - backchannel_injected

Metrics:
  - backchannels_injected: Total played
  - backchannel_suppressed: Total rejected
  - last_backchannel_time: Unix timestamp
  - enabled: Current state

================================================================================
ARCHITECTURE DECISIONS
================================================================================

Why Pause Detection (200-400ms)?
  - <200ms: Natural micro-pauses within speech, not actual pauses
  - 200-400ms: Natural thinking pauses (where humans backchannel)
  - >400ms: Prospect likely finished thought (approaching turn-end)
  - >700ms: Turn-end detected (VAD will trigger)

Why NOT reset VAD/turn timers?
  - Backchannels are acknowledgment, not a full turn
  - If prospect only backchannels (no substantive response), silence manager
    still fires as designed
  - Prevents "hijacking" the conversation flow

Why 8-15s Cooldown?
  - Prevents robotic "yeah yeah yeah" rapid-fire
  - Natural human backchanneling frequency
  - Randomized to avoid predictable pattern

Why Separate Audio Output Path?
  - Backchannel audio queued directly to Twilio (_output_queue)
  - Silence frames yielded to Deepgram STT
  - Allows STT to continue detecting prospect speech
  - Doesn't interrupt ongoing transcription

Why No Conversation History Updates?
  - Backchannels aren't meaningful responses
  - Avoids "polluting" LLM context with non-substantive data
  - LLM doesn't need to know about our backchannels

Performance:
  - Memory: ~150KB (4 audio clips × 300ms max @ 32 bytes/ms)
  - CPU: ~0.1ms per RMS calculation (negligible)
  - Latency: <1ms to queue audio (non-blocking)
  - No impact on STT/LLM/TTS pipeline
